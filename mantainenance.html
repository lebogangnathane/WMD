Pool Table Maintenance Guide

Regular Cleaning

Proper cleaning extends the life of your table and ensures consistent play:

  • Brush the felt weekly with a specialized pool table brush (always brush in one direction)
  • Clean spills immediately with a dry, absorbent cloth (never use water or cleaning solutions)
  • Vacuum under the table monthly to prevent dust accumulation
  • Rotate the cushions occasionally to ensure even wear

Felt Care

The playing surface is the most sensitive part of your table:

  • Rotate the felt every 6-12 months (professionals can re-stretch it to distribute wear)
  • Avoid placing drinks or food near the table
  • Use chalk properly to minimize residue buildup
  • Consider a quality table cover when not in use

Beginner's Guide to Pool

Basic Rules of 8-Ball

The most popular pool game format:

  • Players are assigned either solids (1-7) or stripes (9-15) after the first legal pocket
  • The 8-ball is pocketed last to win
  • Scratching (cue ball pocketed) gives opponent ball-in-hand
  • All shots must be called (ball and pocket) in professional play

Essential Techniques

Fundamentals every player should master:

  • Stance: Balanced with dominant foot slightly forward
  • Bridge: Stable hand formation to guide the cue
  • Stroke: Smooth, straight follow-through
  • English: Using sidespin to control the cue ball
  • Bank Shots: Mastering rail rebounds

Quick Maintenance Checklist

  • Brush felt after every play session
  • Inspect cushions for consistent bounce
  • Clean balls monthly with mild soap
  • Check pocket nets for wear
  • Wipe down rails and frame
  • Examine cues for warping
  • Verify table level every 3 months
  • Replace chalk before it wears down completely
